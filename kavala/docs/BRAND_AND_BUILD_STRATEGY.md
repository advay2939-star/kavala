# KAVALA — Brand, Site & Systems Strategy
### Premium US launch for a rebranded Irimedadi / Arimedadi oral oil
Version 1.0 · July 2026 · Internal document — compliance-reviewed draft

---

## 1. Executive strategy summary

We are not launching an oil; we are launching a ritual. The evidence base for Irimedadi/Arimedadi taila supports one honest story — a traditional Ayurvedic oral-care oil with a small number of promising adjunctive studies and meaningful limitations — and the US regulatory perimeter (FDA intended-use doctrine, FTC substantiation and fake-review rules) makes any disease-treatment framing both illegal and commercially fragile. The winning move is to occupy the empty space between drugstore mouthwash and luxury skincare: a beauty-grade, evidence-literate **botanical oral ritual** brand, priced as a designed object and nightly ceremony rather than a medicine. Premium is earned through packaging, sensory design, restraint, radical transparency about the science, and an operations layer (claims registry, verified reviews, lot tracking, adverse-event intake) that makes the brand safer than its competitors, not louder. Entry at $68, system pricing to $228, subscription refills as the retention engine.

## 2. Brand naming exploration

Criteria: pronounceable in three seconds, ownable in US Class 3/21, hints at mouth/ritual/Ayurveda without sounding pharmaceutical, survives being embossed in small serif type on dark glass.

| # | Name | Logic | Risk |
|---|------|-------|------|
| 1 | **Kavala** | Sanskrit for the oil-holding ritual itself (*kavala graha*). Authentic, short, sounds like a fragrance house. | Shares name with a Greek city (low conflict in oral care). |
| 2 | **Irimé** | Compressed, Frenchified nod to Irimedadi. Keeps lineage, adds chic. | Accent may hurt search/typing. |
| 3 | **Oroveda** | Oro (mouth/gold) + Veda. Legible category signal. | Reads slightly supplement-adjacent. |
| 4 | **Gandusha** | The sibling Sanskrit ritual term. | Harder phonetics for US ear. |
| 5 | **Mukhara** | From *mukha* (mouth). Melodic. | Meaning invisible to US buyer. |
| 6 | **Vedara** | Veda-derived coinage. Smooth. | Generic-coinage energy; weak story. |
| 7 | **Taila House** | Names the material (*taila* = oil). Atelier framing. | "House of X" is crowded. |
| 8 | **Niyama** | Sanskrit for disciplined daily observance. On-message for ritual. | Used by yoga/wellness brands. |
| 9 | **Aranyā** | "Of the forest" — botanical sourcing story. | Distant from oral care. |
| 10 | **Sesa Noir** | Sesame-base + dark-glass aesthetic. Fashion-coded. | Sesa is an existing haircare brand in India. |

### Recommended winner: **KAVALA**
Descriptor line: **Botanical Oral Ritual Oil** (front of pack) / **Ayurvedic-inspired oral-care concentrate** (secondary).

Why it wins: it is the only candidate where the name *is* the product truth — Kavala names the exact traditional practice the product is used for, so the brand story writes itself without a single health claim. It is two syllables of soft consonants (luxury phonetics), visually balanced in caps (KAVALA is a near-palindrome), clears the "does it sound like a drug?" test completely, and leaves Irimedadi available as the heritage formula name on the back of pack: *"Formulated after the classical Irimedadi taila."* Requires standard trademark clearance before launch (flagged in roadmap).

Naming architecture: **KAVALA** (masterbrand) → **No. 01 Ritual Oil** (hero) → future line extensions numbered (No. 02 etc.), never adjective-named ("Whitening", "Repair" are banned by the claims registry).

## 3. Final brand platform

**Positioning statement.** For discerning adults who treat self-care as a designed practice, Kavala is the botanical oral ritual house that turns one minute of oral care into the calmest moment of the day — through a classical Ayurvedic oil formula, beauty-grade design, and unusually honest science.

**Value proposition.** A 1,000-year-old formula, faithfully made and beautifully presented, that adds a sensory, tradition-rooted step to your existing oral-care routine — with every claim we make traceable to a published source you can read yourself.

**Audience segments.**
- *The Ritualist* (30–45, skincare-literate, owns Aesop/Le Labo, buys ceremony): primary. Buys the Ritual Set.
- *The Heritage Buyer* (South Asian diaspora, knows taila culture, currently buys ₹200 pharmacy bottles): trust-rich, price-sensitive on solo bottle; converts on design + gifting.
- *The Optimiser* (biohacker-adjacent, reads studies): converts on the Evidence Library; highest review volume.
- *The Gifter* (seasonal): converts on the Reserve Set and packaging.

**Tone of voice.** British English. Short declaratives. Warm restraint — never breathless, never clinical-cosplay. We say "supports", "part of", "traditionally used"; we never say "fixes", "fights", "kills". We are comfortable stating limits: confidence is quiet.

**Messaging pillars.**
1. *The ritual* — one minute, morning or night; massage or swish; a full sensory sequence.
2. *The formula* — classical Irimedadi lineage; sesame base; named botanicals with monographs.
3. *Honest evidence* — small promising studies, plainly summarised, limitations first-class.
4. *Designed objects* — glass, copper, weight, refills; packaging as the product.
5. *Alongside, never instead* — explicitly additive to brushing, flossing and dental care.

**Compliant claim boundaries (consumer-facing approved list).**
Approved: "freshens breath", "clean-mouth feel", "supports your daily oral-care routine", "traditionally used in Ayurvedic oral care", "a moment of calm", sensory descriptors (warm, clove-spiced, silky).
Restricted (Evidence Library only, fully qualified): summaries of the gingivitis adjunct studies with sample sizes, durations, and limitations; the ADA's position stated verbatim in spirit.
Prohibited everywhere: cure/treat/heal/repair/prevent, pain relief, whitening, anti-gingivitis/anti-caries/antibacterial/anti-inflammatory as product claims, "clinical-grade", "dentist-approved", any FDA implication, any suggestion of replacing brushing/flossing/dental visits.

## 5. Pricing strategy

| Product | Price | Anchor logic |
|---|---|---|
| No. 01 Ritual Oil | **$68** | Above all oil-pulling commodity ($10–40), at luxury-skincare serum parity; glass + pipette + carton justify it visually before copy does |

| The Reserve Set | **$228** | Ceiling anchor that makes $148 feel considered |

Justification: the category's price ceiling collapses only when a lone bottle asks for medical money. Every price above $68 buys *objects and system*, not stronger claims. Psychological notes: whole-dollar pricing (no .99 — luxury code); the $228 anchor exists partly to sell the $148 set; subscription discount framed as "member pricing", never countdown timers or fake stock scarcity (numbered Reserve runs are real scarcity). Free shipping threshold at $100 nudges Duo.

## 9. Key site pages — outlines and core copy

- **The Ritual.** Founder-story framework (see below) → the nightly sequence in cinematic photography → the sensory profile (warm, clove-forward, faintly astringent) → objects. Copy stays in ceremony territory; zero efficacy language.
- **Ingredients.** One monograph per botanical: Latin name, Sanskrit name, traditional role in the classical formula, sensory contribution, sourcing note. Header: `Named, sourced, explained.` Allergen callout: sesame base, clove (eugenol) — patch-test guidance, discontinue-if-irritated line.
- **Evidence & Tradition.** Structure: (1) The tradition — kavala/gandusha in classical texts. (2) The modern studies — three plain-language study cards (45-patient/21-day chlorhexidine comparison; 100-patient adjunct-to-scaling study; recession study where surgery outperformed). Each card: population, duration, finding, *Limitations* section (mandatory, plain language). (3) The wider picture — oil-pulling evidence is mixed; the ADA does not recommend it and notes no reliable studies show cavity reduction, whitening, or general oral-health improvement. (4) What this means for you — Kavala is used *alongside* standard care; not intended to diagnose, treat, cure or prevent disease. (5) Sources — full citations.
- **How to Use.** Six illustrated steps; timing; storage; who should not use it (sesame/clove sensitivity, children, pregnancy — consult a professional); the mandatory line: `Kavala is not a substitute for professional dental diagnosis or treatment. Tooth pain needs a dentist, promptly.`
- **Reviews.** Verified-purchase badge system, disclosure labels, honest empty state pre-launch. No placeholder testimonials, ever.
- **Shop.** Product ladder with restrained cards; PDP includes ingredients accordion, usage, shipping, and the disclaimer above the buy box, not below the fold.
- **FAQ.** Ships with the hard questions answered honestly: "Will this whiten my teeth?" → `We make no whitening claims and the evidence doesn't support them.` "Can it replace mouthwash/brushing?" → `No.` "Why is it $68?" → the design/objects/sourcing answer, stated plainly.
- **Contact.** Support form + dedicated **product experience / adverse reaction** category feeding the AE intake queue (MoCRA posture).
- **Legal set.** Privacy, Terms, Shipping, Returns (30-day, opened-bottle policy stated), Medical Disclaimer (standalone page mirroring the footer line with expanded guidance), Accessibility Statement (WCAG 2.2 AA target, contact route).

**Founder-story framework (compliant).** Grandmother's brass bottle of taila in a Pune household → the gap between how the ritual *felt* and how it was packaged → a pilgrimage through classical texts and modern papers → the decision to make the honest, beautiful edition. Emotional arc rests on memory and craft, never on a health transformation narrative.

## 10. Motion & interaction system

- **Hero:** single 6s light-sweep across bottle glass on load (CSS transform on a gradient mask), then still. One orchestrated moment, no loops.
- **Scroll choreography:** sections fade-and-rise 12px, 500ms, cubic-bezier(0.22,1,0.36,1), triggered at 20% viewport, once. The Minute section: three frames pin briefly and cross-fade.
- **Hover:** product cards lift 2px + oil-meniscus underline draws left→right 300ms; nav links use the meniscus underline; buttons darken 80ms.
- **Reveal timing:** 60–90ms stagger between siblings; never more than 3 staggered children.
- **Loading:** skeletons in bone-on-lacquer; buy-button spinner replaces label, width preserved.
- **Cart:** right drawer, 320ms slide + scrim fade; added item does a single 1.02 scale pulse.
- **Reduced motion:** all of the above collapse to opacity-only under `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Banned: parallax, marquee, scroll-jacking, cursor followers, confetti.

## 11. Visual design system

**Colour** (avoiding the generic cream-and-terracotta default; palette derived from the material itself — dark sesame oil in amber glass):
- `lacquer` #171210 — near-black warm brown; primary background
- `bone` #F2ECE2 — light surface / primary text on dark
- `oil` #A9682B — burnished amber accent (the oil itself); links, meniscus, CTAs
- `clay` #E5DBCB — secondary light surface
- `ink` #2B221B — text on light
- `copper` #8C5A3A — secondary accent (tongue-cleaner metal), used sparingly

**Typography.** Display: **Bodoni Moda** (high-contrast didone — fashion-house authority, tightly tracked, never below 28px). Body/UI: **Instrument Sans** (quiet grotesque). Labels/eyebrows: Instrument Sans, 11px, +0.18em tracking, small caps feel. Type scale: 12/14/16/20/28/40/56/76.

**Spacing scale:** 4-base — 4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96, 128. Sections breathe at 96–128px desktop, 64 mobile.

**Component language:** hairline borders (1px, 12% opacity), radius 2px (near-square = object-like), no drop shadows on light surfaces, one soft shadow tier on dark. **Signature element:** the *oil meniscus* — a 2px amber gradient line with a subtle specular highlight, used as section divider, hover underline, and progress indicator. It is the one decorative flourish; everything else stays silent.

**Packaging cues:** amber glass, bone label with lacquer type, embossed KAVALA, batch/lot number printed visibly (transparency as luxury), linen-textured rigid boxes, copper foil only on the Reserve edition.

**Imagery:** macro glass and oil surfaces, warm single-source light, deep shadows, hands in frame (ritual), no smiling-model stock, no clinical whites, no before/after imagery (banned by claims registry).

## 12. Frontend architecture

- **Framework:** Next.js 15 (App Router, RSC) + TypeScript strict + Tailwind CSS v4 + Framer Motion (client islands only). Justification over alternatives: RSC keeps the marketing pages static/edge-cached (LCP < 1.5s), commerce mutations run as server actions, and the team already lives in this stack.
- **Folder structure:**
```
app/            (routes; route groups: (marketing), (shop), (legal), admin/)
components/     ui/ · marketing/ · shop/ · admin/
lib/            content/ · commerce/ · compliance/ · analytics/ · utils.ts · types.ts
db/             schema.sql · seed.ts
emails/         react-email templates
```
- **Component tree (homepage):** `Header → Hero → Meniscus → TheMinute → FormulaTriptych → HonestyBlock → ObjectsLadder(ProductCard×) → RitualNotReplacement → ReviewsBlock(EmptyState) → SubscribeStrip → Footer(Disclaimer)`.
- **State:** server-first; cart in a Zustand store hydrated from a signed cookie cart-id; no global client state beyond cart + UI.
- **SEO:** static metadata per route, OG images generated per product, semantic landmarks, sitemap/robots, canonical domains.
- **Schema markup:** `Organization`, `Product` + `Offer` (price, availability), `FAQPage`, `BreadcrumbList`. `AggregateRating` emitted **only** when real verified reviews exist (compliance-gated in code).

## 13. Backend architecture

- **Commerce:** Medusa v2 (self-hosted, Postgres) — full control over the subscription + claims-adjacent data without Shopify app sprawl; Stripe for payments + Stripe Billing for subscriptions (pause/skip via customer portal + custom endpoints).
- **CMS:** Payload 3 (same Next.js repo, same Postgres) — ingredients, evidence entries, claims registry, legal docs, home modules as typed collections.
- **Auth:** Payload auth for admin (RBAC); customer auth via Medusa + email magic links (Resend).
- **Orders:** Medusa order pipeline → webhook → internal `orders` mirror for support notes + lot allocation.
- **Analytics/eventing:** PostHog (product analytics + session replay with masking), server-side event relay for purchase events; Sentry both tiers; Resend + react-email for transactional.
- **Event taxonomy:** `view_item`, `add_to_cart`, `begin_checkout`, `purchase`, `subscribe_start`, `subscription_pause/cancel`, `evidence_page_read` (scroll ≥ 75% — a trust KPI), `review_submitted`.

## 14. Admin dashboard spec

Sections (left rail): **Orders** (search, status, lot allocation, refund via Stripe, support-note thread) · **Subscriptions** (state machine view, dunning, pause/skip log) · **Inventory** (per-SKU, per-lot quantities, low-stock thresholds) · **Content** (Payload collections: pages, ingredients, FAQs) · **Claims Registry** (every marketing string with status approved/restricted/prohibited, linked evidence, approver, audit log — new site copy cannot publish while containing strings matching prohibited patterns) · **Evidence Library** (studies with source class + confidence) · **Reviews** (moderation queue, verified-purchase flag auto-set, disclosure toggles, rejection reasons logged) · **Support** (ticket inbox; category = adverse reaction escalates) · **Adverse Events** (intake record: product, lot, description, severity, reporter contact, actions taken, MoCRA-serious flag + export) · **Lots** (batch no., MFG/EXP, supplier COA file, linked orders — one-click affected-customer list for recalls) · **Settings/Roles**.

**RBAC:** `owner` (all) · `ops` (orders, inventory, lots, support) · `content` (CMS drafts; publish requires compliance role for claim-bearing fields) · `compliance` (claims registry, evidence, AE, review-moderation veto) · `support` (tickets, order read).

## 15. Data schema (implemented in `db/schema.sql`)

Core tables: `products`, `variants` (SKU, price, weight), `inventory_levels` (variant × lot), `lots` (batch, mfg/exp, COA URL), `customers`, `orders`, `order_items` (variant + lot allocation), `subscriptions` (status, cadence, stripe_sub_id), `reviews` (order_id FK proves verified purchase; status pending/approved/rejected; disclosure flags), `evidence_entries` (citation, source_class, population, duration, findings, limitations, confidence), `claims` (text, channel, status approved/restricted/prohibited, evidence_entry FK, approved_by, audit timestamps), `legal_documents` (versioned, effective dates), `support_tickets`, `adverse_events` (severity, lot FK, mocra_serious, reported_at, actions), `admin_users` + `roles`. Full DDL with constraints ships in the repo.

## 16. Retention flows (Resend + react-email)

1. **Welcome** (T0): the story in 150 words, what arrives in the box, one CTA. No discount begging.
2. **Ritual onboarding** (delivery + 1d, +4d, +10d): how to begin; the sensory guide; "the honest science" email that links the Evidence page — trust as retention.
3. **Review request** (delivery + 21d): verified link, no incentive tied to sentiment, disclosure-compliant.
4. **Refill reminder** (day 45 of 56-day cycle): bottle-level illustration, one-tap refill or subscribe.
5. **Win-back** (lapsed 120d): one beautiful email, one honest offer (free Refill sleeve upgrade, not a discount scream); then silence — luxury doesn't chase.

## 17. Verified review system

Collection only via post-purchase tokenised links (order-bound). `verified_purchase` computed, never editable. Moderation: approve/reject with logged reason; edits limited to typo-level with original preserved; incentivised reviews (if ever) disclosed by badge and never conditioned on sentiment. UI: rating distribution, newest-first, verified badge, "how reviews work" explainer link. Empty state before launch — no seeded content. `AggregateRating` schema only from approved verified rows.

## 18. Evidence library design

Each entry: citation → **source class** (RCT / clinical comparative / review / tradition) → population, duration → findings (plain language) → **limitations (mandatory)** → confidence (promising-but-limited / mixed / insufficient). Claims registry maps each marketing string to entries: **approved** (safe cosmetic/sensory claims), **restricted** (evidence-page-only, must render with limitations component), **prohibited** (build-time lint blocks publish). This is the moat: the brand cannot drift into non-compliance even under future marketing pressure.

## 21. QA checklist

Compliance: no prohibited strings anywhere (automated scan) · disclaimer present on every route · evidence pages render limitations · reviews empty state honest · no AggregateRating without data. Accessibility: WCAG 2.2 AA, keyboard-complete, focus visible, reduced-motion path, contrast ≥ 4.5:1 (bone on lacquer = 12.9:1). Performance: LCP < 1.5s, CLS < 0.05, images AVIF + sized. Commerce: cart persistence, tax/shipping calc, 3DS, subscription pause/skip/cancel, refund path, webhook idempotency. Content: no lorem ipsum, allergen notices, British English pass. Ops: AE escalation test, lot→orders recall query test, RBAC matrix test.
